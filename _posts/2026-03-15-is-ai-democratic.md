Last week one of my coworkers said something along the lines of:  
"Everyone can use AI, therefore the equality of power is higher than ever!"

Since that moment, this statement has been popping up periodically in my mind, and I am not sure whether I agree or disagree.

It is true that even with the free subscriptions from the big AI companies, you can solve problems at a pace you wouldn't even have dared to imagine before AI. Everyone can create a free account and use it. Only if you need more usage or power do you have to pay, but for daily use, the free models are sufficient.

But on the other hand, not everyone has the capability to use AI with the same efficiency. You need to have the time and mental capacity to know when and how to use it. Mindlessly prompting and trusting the results will lead you nowhere.

So even if everyone has the same tools, I could imagine that privileged people with better education will use these tools more efficiently and effectively, which could result in an even bigger gap in power.

## SaaS provider

Even worse, in my opinion, is the corporate angle of this topic.

This whole "Everyone can use it, so it is democratic" argument reminds me of the widely believed myth of the early internet: that with the internet, there would be no need for monopolies anymore because everyone could create their own stores and services.

But reality sometimes hits hard, and the opposite happened. There are more and larger monopolies or quasi-monopolies than ever before. And the same could happen with AI.

Only the biggest and wealthiest companies can afford the computing power needed to run an LLM as a service. And it gets worse by the minute. Due to the heavy demand for more and more computing power for AI, the demand for hardware like RAM rises and, simultaneously, the prices.

So even if you have an affinity for tech and want to self-host an open-source LLM to avoid being dependent on giant corporations and to keep control over your data, it will still be very expensive.
